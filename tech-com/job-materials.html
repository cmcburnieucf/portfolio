Job Materials

The idea behind the first project of ENC3241, "Job Materials", was to create a professional resume and cover letter for a given job posting. Through this assignment, students, such as myself, learn that some forms of technical communication are concerned about getting an idea across to the audience by understanding what the same audience is looking for. Being concise and utlizing certain words and phrases to assist in displaying an idea is more important than writing more to meet a word count -- a lesson that was not taught in my earlier writing classes. Below is the given scenario that revised documents, further below, were based on.

Resume

Since I knew how to create a resume, I based the one designed for this project on the others I have developed in the past. The job posting was for a software engineer intern, so I tailored the resume such that the individual viewing the document would know I could be qualified to fill in the role. As the original resume had a minor issue with formatting in one of the sections, I revised the document to fix that. I also decided to further showcase my ability to be a software engineer intern by including a new project, the first one underneath the "Projects" section. I was not able to include it before because the team had barely started on development. Below is the revised resume.
