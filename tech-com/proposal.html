Research Proposal Project

Demonstrating rigorous research planning and professional proposal writing

Assignment Overview

This assignment required creating a professional research grant proposal modeled after the UCF Office of Undergraduate Research's Student Research Grant format. The proposal needed to clearly articulate a research question, methodology, expected outcomes, and budget justification within specific section requirements and word limits.

Key Objectives

  • Develop a compelling research question with academic significance
  • Design rigorous research methods appropriate for the field
  • Create a realistic budget justification within $1,500 limit
  • Maintain professional tone and formatting throughout
  • Synthesize relevant literature to contextualize the research

The proposal format followed strict section requirements including a project title, objective statement, background and significance explanation, research methods description, expected outcomes analysis, literature review, preliminary work summary, IRB/IACUC statement, and detailed budget.

Technical Communication Insights

Audience Awareness in Research Proposals

This project taught me that successful research proposals must simultaneously address multiple audiences with different priorities. Review committees typically include both specialists in your field who evaluate technical merit, and administrators who assess feasibility and alignment with institutional goals. My proposal needed to balance detailed methodology for experts with clear significance statements for generalists.

I learned to use executive summaries strategically, highlight interdisciplinary relevance, and frame technical content within broader impacts. Future proposals will benefit from even more explicit signaling of content importance to different reader types.

Structural Requirements

The strict section requirements demonstrated how standardized formats improve review efficiency while challenging writers to connect discrete components into a coherent narrative. I developed strategies for:

  • Maintaining flow despite standardized headings
  • Creating internal references between sections
  • Balancing repetition with new information
  • Meeting word count restrictions without sacrificing clarity

Budgets as Persuasive Documents

Creating the budget section revealed financial justification as a form of technical rhetoric. Each expense required:

  • Clear connection to research activities
  • Evidence of cost-effectiveness
  • Appropriate categorization
  • Alignment with institutional guidelines
